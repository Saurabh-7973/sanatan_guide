Chapter I · Verse 1
धृतराष्ट्र उवाच ।
धर्मक्षेत्रे कुरुक्षेत्रे
समवेता युयुत्सवः ।
मामकाः पाण्डवाश्चैव
किमकुर्वत सञ्जय ॥
Transliteration
dhṛtarāṣṭra uvāca .
dharmakṣetre kurukṣetre samavetā yuyutsavaḥ .
māmakāḥ pāṇḍavāścaiva kimakurvata sañjaya ||1-1||
Translation
Dhṛtarāṣṭra said: O Sañjaya, what did my sons and the sons of Pāṇḍu actually do when, desirous of battle, they had assembled together on the holy plain of Kurukṣetra?
— Swami Sivananda

Commentary
AI · Gemini

The Gītā opens with a question, not a sermon. Dhṛtarāṣṭra is the blind king — and the blindness is more than physical. He cannot see the war his ambition has caused. He cannot see his sons.

The very first word, dharmakṣetre, "in the field of dharma," is the key. Kurukṣetra is not just a battlefield — it is the place where dharma is decided. Every choice you make is on this same field.

What changed and why

  • Removed the orange flower-yantra and the BG bubble. Both were generic decoration. The verse coordinate ("Chapter I · Verse 1") now lives as inscriptional small caps inside the leaf — it carries the heritage signal.
  • Removed the "Translation on" pill, "BHAGAVAD GITA" vertical ribbon, "CH 1 V 1" stack, bottom verse label, and bottom edit pencil. All chrome, no information.
  • The leaf is now the entire reading surface. Top + bottom binding lines frame the Sanskrit. Margins match real palm-leaf folio proportions. Continuity with Home: same diamond, same rule, same saffron.
  • Tap-a-word reveals meaning in a callout. Devanāgarī + IAST + English meaning + grammar (case/number). Word selection draws a thin saffron underline; rest of the screen dims to 45% so attention stays on the chosen word.
  • Translation moved to flowing left-aligned serif prose (per Geeta Press / Chinmaya conventions). Centered translation reads like a quote-card; flowing prose reads like a book.
  • "Explain this verse" added as the natural next layer. A specific contextual question — not a generic "Ask AI" button. When expanded, becomes a Commentary card with follow-up prompts.
  • Progress rail under the topbar. "1 / 47 — 2%" makes location-in-chapter glanceable. Hairline thin, never decorative.
  • Bottom utility bar consolidates Translation switcher, Listen (audio), Notes, and prev/next. Five things in one strip instead of five separate UI elements scattered across the screen.
