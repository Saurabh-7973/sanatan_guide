What changed and why

  • Replaced "Itihasa & Purana" sections with the full Śruti / Smṛti taxonomy. Six families: Śruti, Itihāsa, Purāṇa, Darśana, Dharmaśāstra, Tamil. This is how every actual Sanskrit library on earth is organized — mirroring it makes the app feel like a real repository, not a content app.
  • Cards became rows. Cards say "product." Rows say "catalog." A scripture library is the latter. Net result: 2-3x more scriptures visible at a glance and a quieter, more navigable surface.
  • Vedas get a 2×2 grid. The four Vedas are parallel (Ṛg, Sāma, Yajur, Atharva), not sequential. Geometric layout reflects that. Cell aspect ratio is 3:2 — Sulba Sutra brick proportion from Vedic altars.
  • Library header flexes the scale. "1,33,613 verses · 31 scriptures" set in serif italic numerals. Indian-format comma grouping (1,33,613 not 133,613) — small but specific.
  • Family headers are typographically authoritative. Devanāgarī display + italic English subtitle + saffron rule + small descriptor. Readable in two seconds, organizing in one.
  • Removed the Devanāgarī letter-circles. "गी" before Bhagavad Gītā wasn't an icon, it was a placeholder. Replaced with a tiny diamond family-glyph (◆ for Smṛti, ◇ for Śruti). One character, monochrome, never decorative.
  • Reading state surfaced at the library level. "1 verse read" appears in saffron next to the meta line. You see your progress on every text without drilling in.
  • Search is inline, not a separate screen. Tap → expand → results replace the family list → "esc" returns you. Highlighting on the matched substring ("Bhagavad Gītā").
  • Tamil corpus rendered in actual Tamil script (திருக்குறள்). Not Devanagari. Respect for the linguistic tradition — the Tirukkuṟaḷ was never written in Devanagari.
  • Family headers stick on scroll. No matter how deep you are, you always know which family you're browsing.
