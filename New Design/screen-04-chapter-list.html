What changed and why

  • Removed the dotted progress strip across the top. Eighteen dots representing 18 chapters was unreadable as data and arbitrary as decoration. The single chapter-count line in the header carries the same information more clearly.
  • Reduced "Bhagavad Gita" header to a compact two-line block. Sanskrit display + italic English subtitle + meta line. No more dominant title that the user has already navigated past.
  • Resume row added at the top. When the user has read at least one verse, the chapter they were last in becomes the dominant action — saffron leaf-thread on the left edge, gradient saffron background trailing into the row. One-time pulse animation on screen entry — like the thread catching candlelight.
  • Devanāgarī numerals for chapter numbers (१, २, ३ ...). With small Arabic numeral underneath as a literacy fallback. This is the most distinctive heritage detail on the screen — never decorative, always functional.
  • Dual-language chapter titles. Devanāgarī chapter title (अर्जुनविषादयोग) + italic English translation underneath. Mirrors Geeta Press / Chinmaya Mission print convention. Information-dense without clutter.
  • Per-chapter progress as a hairline. Replaces the empty circular ring that communicated nothing. 1.5px saffron fill on a track underneath the verse-count line. Visible only when the user has actually started a chapter.
  • Reading time per chapter ("~12 min"). Habit users plan around time, not verse count. Computed from verse count × user's read pace.
  • Completed chapters get a checkmark and de-emphasized type. "Read" chapters fade slightly so the eye finds the unread ones first. Standard pattern, executed cleanly.
  • Empty state preserved by the absence of resume. First-time visit just shows the chapter list. No empty-state illustration, no "start reading" CTA — chapter 1 is the natural first row.
