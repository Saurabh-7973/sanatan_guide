What does the Gītā say about anger?

Anger is treated in the Gītā not as a sin but as a corruption of judgment. Krishna traces a chain: attachment to objects breeds desire; obstructed desire becomes anger; anger destroys clarity; loss of clarity destroys memory; loss of memory ruins the intellect; and a ruined intellect destroys the self.

‖२·६२‖ ‖२·६३‖
क्रोधाद्भवति सम्मोहः सम्मोहात्स्मृतिविभ्रमः ।
"From anger arises delusion; from delusion, the bewilderment of memory..."

The Gītā's remedy is not suppression but tracing the chain backward — to act without attachment to results. The Bhāgavata adds that anger can be transmuted, that what burns the worldly self can illumine the spiritual one when its energy is redirected toward truth.

What's covered here

AI Chat — general mode

  • No verse anchor at the top. Replaced with a compact title block: small ॐ glyph + "ASK THE PANDIT" small-caps. This is what distinguishes general mode from verse-anchored mode (which has the full Sanskrit incipit + leaf-thread at top).
  • Empty state hero: large ॐ + question + sub-prose + 4 example questions. The 4 examples teach the user what general mode is for: concepts ("anger"), life questions ("death"), comparisons ("dharma vs karma"), reading guidance ("which Upaniṣad first").
  • Suggest chips are full italic-serif questions, not single words. Shows the user that questions are conversational, not keyword-based.
  • Same message styling as verse-anchored mode. User = saffron-tinted rounded bubble right-aligned. AI = flowing Lora italic prose, no bubble. Citation cards inline use the binding-line motif from the rest of the app.
  • "New conversation" + icon top-right (visible in conversation state). Clears history, returns to empty state. Not present on empty state (nothing to clear).
  • Input placeholder: "Ask anything about the texts..." — distinct from verse-anchored placeholder ("Ask about this verse...").

Notes bottom sheet

  • Modal slide-up from bottom. Drag handle at top, 16px radius on top corners. 55% black scrim dims the verse behind.
  • Header: "Your note" + verse coordinate (‖२·४७‖) on the right in saffron. The coord ties the note to its verse explicitly.
  • Lora italic textarea. 14.5px, line-height 1.65. The italic-serif aesthetic matches commentary and AI replies — notes are personal reflections, the literary register of the app.
  • 200 character limit. Counter at bottom-right. Forces brevity; encourages reflection over essays. Notes are journal-style, not long-form.
  • Action row: Delete (tertiary, left) · Cancel (secondary, right) · Save (primary, right). Delete only shows when editing an existing note. Save disabled until content exists.
  • One note per verse. Simpler model than threaded notes. If user wants multiple thoughts, they edit existing.
  • Notes appear on Bookmarks screen under each saved verse in italic-serif (already designed in Screen 07).

Share bottom sheet

  • Three-segment format chooser at the top. "Sanskrit only / + Translation / Full citation" — gives the user control without overwhelming. Middle option is default (Sanskrit + Translation).
  • Live preview card with palm-leaf binding line. Shows exactly what will be shared, formatted with the same heritage vocabulary as Verse Detail. This is the share artifact's identity.
  • Deep link included: sanatanguide.app/BG/2/47. Recipients who don't have the app see a web preview; recipients with the app deep-link directly to the verse.
  • Two CTAs: "Copy text" (secondary) · "Share" (primary). Copy puts the formatted text on clipboard. Share invokes the system share sheet with the same text payload.
  • v1 ships text-only. The image-card share (with full design, deity colors, etc. — like Instagram-friendly cards) is documented as v2 in V2_BACKLOG.md. Don't build it for launch.
  • Format strings (canonical, do not change):
    Sanskrit only: {deva}\n\n— {scripture} {coord}\n{deep_link}
    + Translation: {deva}\n\n"{translation}"\n\n— {scripture} {coord}\n{deep_link}
    Full: {deva}\n\n{transliteration}\n\n"{translation}"\n\n— {scripture} {coord} ({attribution})\n{deep_link}
