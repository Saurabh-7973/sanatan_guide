What changed and why

  • Renamed "Saved Verses" to "Pothī" with English subtitle "Your collection." A pothī is the actual word for a personal manuscript bundle in the Sanskrit/Hindi tradition. Every other reading app says "Saved Verses." Yours says what the thing actually is.
  • Each saved verse is a leaf, not a card. Top binding line + central diamond hole + saffron knot-mark in the upper right (the silk thread that ties the leaf to mark significance). Compresses the binding-line vocabulary from Verse Detail into a smaller surface.
  • Sanskrit incipit visible, not buried. Your current bookmark card shows only a fragment ("धृतराष्ट्र उवाच |"). The redesign shows a fuller incipit — enough that the user recognizes the verse without tapping in.
  • Personal notes — the highest-leverage feature most reading apps don't have. A 1-line italic-serif note appears below the verse, separated by a dashed rule. "The line my father read aloud at his retirement." Turns bookmarks from a list of saves into personal commentary that compounds in value over time.
  • Sort tabs: Recent / By scripture. Solves the heavy-user problem (50+ bookmarks). The "By scripture" view groups leaves under scripture headers. Filter affordance on the right for future tag/collection work.
  • Empty state actually teaches. Three abstract leaves stacked on a saffron thread illustration. Title: "Your pothī awaits." Body explains that bookmarking is more than saving — you can add notes about why a verse spoke to you. CTA: "Begin reading."
  • Leaf footer carries scripture name + saved date. Replaces the floating "BG.1.1" pill and the calendar-icon-with-date. Cleaner, more readable, no chrome.
  • Devanāgarī verse coordinates throughout. "२·४७" instead of "BG.1.1" — system continuity with Verse List, Verse Detail, and Search.
  • Design implies long-term use. The pothī framing scales from 1 leaf to 500. The sort tabs only matter at scale. The personal-note feature compounds in value over years. This is a screen designed to be used, not just shipped.
