Home screen — three design directions

Each direction uses your existing tokens (Saffron #E8820C, warm cream / near-black surfaces, Tiro Devanagari, Lora, Outfit) and preserves all current content: greeting, verse of the day, panchang, festival, learning path, continue reading, streak, and the 3-tab nav. Click Light / Dark on each frame to compare.

Direction 01
Illuminated Manuscript
The home screen as a hand-bound folio. Italic running heads, a ruled saffron margin, a drop-cap translation, and ornamental rules that echo a medieval codex. Typography does all the heavy lifting — no illustrations, no gradients. It makes the app feel like an edition you keep on a shelf, not a product.

Direction 02
Temple Dawn
A horizon of saffron light rises behind the verse. The day's almanac becomes three celestial tiles — sun for vāra, a live lunar phase for tithi, a small mandala for parva. Soft translucent surfaces and a warm radial glow give the screen the quiet atmosphere of the hour before the first bell at a temple.

Direction 03
Sacred Minimalism
One verse. Centered. Breathing. The rest of the home — almanac, path, streak — collapses into a compact bottom sheet. A single saffron accent at a time. The screen is almost entirely negative space, which is what makes the verse feel like something to sit with rather than scroll past.
