What changed and why

  • Curriculum is primary; streak is secondary. Your current screen gives the 7×6 streak grid ~50% of the viewport. The redesign compresses the streak to a 7-day week-strip (M-T-W-T-F-S-S dots, ~70px tall), and surfaces a Continue anchor for the next module — same vocabulary as Resume on Chapter List and Verse List.
  • The dotted "path connector" became a real binding-thread. A continuous saffron-deep hairline runs down the left margin, with a small diamond knot at each module — same diamond hole vocabulary as the binding line on verse cards. Filled saffron for completed modules, outlined for locked, glowing for the active/next one.
  • Killed the big numbered tile (1, 2, 8 in rounded squares). Replaced with a small serif numeral after the knot. The number is just an index; the title is what the user reads.
  • "I — Foundations" became "I. Foundations" with proper section header. Section status moves to the right ("In progress" / "Locked" pill). Description sits below. Progress bar with saffron fill. Cleaner, more scannable.
  • Streak tap → bottom sheet calendar. The full month grid lives in a bottom sheet, not as primary screen real estate. Returning users glance at the week strip; users curious about the month tap to expand.
  • Continue anchor with saffron leaf-thread. Same vocabulary as Chapter List Resume, Verse List Resume, AI Chat anchor, Onboarding selection. Fifth time this thread appears across the system — by now it consistently means "your place / your active."
  • "III. Mastery" horizon at the bottom. Even though it's not built, the dashed horizon card hints at what comes after Deepening — "The Itihāsa-Purāṇa corpus, the six Darśanas, and the philosophical schools — when you are ready." Tells users the curriculum has scope; the journey doesn't end at Deepening.
  • Locked-state UX is honest. Locked modules show full title and description (not greyed into oblivion) — users want to know what's coming. Tapping a locked module produces a contextual toast: "Complete 3 more Foundations modules to unlock Deepening." No hidden information.
  • Sub-section progress hairline beneath each section header. 1.5px saffron fill on a track. Same vocabulary as per-chapter progress on Chapter List. The user always knows how far through any section they are.
