What changed and why

  • Killed one of the two redundant level screens. Your current flow has "Where would you like to begin? (Complete Beginner / Curious Hindu / Serious Seeker)" AND "How familiar are you? (Beginner / Regular / Scholar)" — two screens for one decision. The redesign keeps the cleaner Beginner/Regular/Scholar framing and merges it onto the Welcome screen.
  • Removed "Curious Hindu" identity framing. The old card said "I grew up Hindu but want to learn deeper" — assumed user identity in a way the others didn't. The redesign keeps levels purely about familiarity with scriptures, agnostic to the user's background.
  • Welcome and level selection on one screen. Logomark + tagline + question + 3 cards + Continue all fit comfortably in a 390×844 viewport. Two extra taps deleted from onboarding.
  • Two screens total: Welcome+Level, then Daily Reminder. Down from three. Every screen between download and first verse is friction; this is the v1 ceiling.
  • "॥ श्री गणेशाय नमः ॥" as a tiny watermark above the step dots. Traditional opening invocation; signals intent without dominating. Same line shown in the first-day Home empty state — system continuity.
  • Saffron leaf-thread on the selected card. Same vocabulary as Resume rows (Chapter List, Verse List, AI Chat anchor). The user has now seen this thread three times in their first 30 seconds — it becomes a learned visual that means "your place" / "your selection."
  • Reminder screen frames the why, not just the permission. "A verse a day · A short morning notification with one verse — chosen for the day." The user sees the value before being asked to commit. System notification permission only fires on tap of "Enable reminder."
  • Time picker on the reminder screen, not buried in Settings. User can choose 7AM (default) or change before enabling. Reduces "I enabled it but it's at the wrong time" abandonment.
  • "Skip for now" / "Not now" on every screen. Onboarding is fully skippable. Skipped users get sensible defaults (Regular level, no reminder). Both choices respected.
  • Step dots, not chevron-back. Two saffron dots at the top show progress (1 of 2, 2 of 2). No "back" on the first screen because there's nothing to go back to.
  • The logomark is typographic, not pictorial. Saffron ॐ + serif "Sanatan Guide" + italic tagline. No deity image, no mandala, no lotus. Sets the visual register for the whole app: typography over imagery.
