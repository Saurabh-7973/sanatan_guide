What changed and why

  • Daṇḍa-marked verse numbers (‖१‖, ‖२‖...). The double-vertical-bar is the verse-end mark in every Sanskrit manuscript — using it as the verse-number container is functional, not decorative. Instantly recognizable to Sanskrit readers; quietly distinctive to everyone else.
  • Sanskrit incipit dominates the row, English helps recognition. Reversed the previous hierarchy — Sanskrit is primary (where the verse lives), English is the recognition aid in italic serif underneath.
  • Compact two-line chapter header instead of the previous 100px-tall block. Sanskrit chapter title + chapter number (right-aligned) on line 1, English subtitle on line 2, hairline progress beneath. Fits in <90px including padding.
  • Resume anchor surfaces the next unread verse. Same saffron leaf-thread treatment as Chapter List for system continuity. Shows the actual verse incipit, not just "verse 2."
  • Verse jumper for long chapters. Sticky right-edge column with Devanāgarī decade markers (१, ११, २१, ३१...). Drag to scroll, with a saffron tooltip showing the verse you're hovering at. Like iOS Contacts alpha-scroll, but with verse decades. Essential for the Mahābhārata's 300-verse chapters.
  • Sub-section headers ("Verses 11—20 · Sāṅkhya Begins"). For very long chapters, breaks the list into traditional verse-decade sections. Editor-curated section labels add literary navigation: "where does the philosophy actually start?"
  • Removed the bookmark icon column entirely. Empty bookmark icons next to 46 unbookmarked verses were pure noise. Now: bookmarked verses show a small saffron bookmark; everything else shows nothing. The state of "not bookmarked" doesn't need a UI element.
  • Three glance-level states: read / unread / bookmarked. Read verses get a small saffron checkmark and slightly de-emphasized type. Bookmarked verses get a saffron bookmark icon. Unread is the default — no marker. Three states, one column.
  • Density increased. Verse rows compress to ~58px (vs ~70px on Chapter List). Users scanning 47 or 300 verses need density; users scanning 18 chapters can afford breathing room.
